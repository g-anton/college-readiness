Guidance to College Readiness

Are you a freshman in highschool who is starting to think about college? A student who feels scared to dive into the "adult" world. A student who needs a bit of guidance well this is checklist for you! You will be provided with tips/tricks to ease the college experience. At the end you will be to download a PNG version of the checklist to keep with you through your college prep journey.

Start by speaking with your advisor about the possibility of taking accelerated math courses. Studies have shown students who partake in higher level, rigorous courses are more likely to further their academic succes in college. Consider taking Alegbra 1 to then move onto to a calculus track to prepare.

Not only is it a good idea for you to discuss math courses but talk about Advance Placement classes too. These are college level courses applicable for college credit if you pass with a "3" or higher. Many college and universities recognize the credits which go towards earning your associates degree (AA) in a shorter time-frame. Another way to recieve college credits during highschool is through Dual Enrollment offered by your high school, local college, or online. Dual Enrollment courses are open to tenth, eleventh, and twelfh graders. The credit earned applies to both high school and and college requirements. The best part is the courses are available for free to students in public, private, and home education programs.

After speaking with your advisor set up a meeting with a tutor. This will be benefical if you find the coursework to be a bit of a challenge. A tutor will assit you not only with assignments but provide you with additional tools for overall structure. This is a great resource to prepare for college!

If your school does not offer tutoring servies another tool would be searching the internet for free resources. A great resource is Schoolhouse.world created by the CEO of Khan Academy. An opportunity for students to join online group study sessions, SAT bootcamps, and live homework help everyday.

Advisor can collaborate make sure that all students have equitable access to a college counseling program that encourages the culture of going to college. Speaking with your advisor is highly recommended because they're the ones who know all the information about your major, like what kind of classes you'll need to take. Every semester you should schedule a meeting with your advisor, so you can stay on track with your classes. You don't want to spend extra money on classes.

It is important to talk to other peers about what they understand and want to know about college. Consider join clubs or collaborate groups to help ease the transition when starting your first college semester. Communication is key to not getting overwhelmed when it comes to school and learning how to balance everything is helpful to making it an easy and stress-free semester.

A tip for when it is time to pick classes both in high school and college!

It is important for students to choose their classes before their enrollment date. Atleast a week or two prior to your enrollment date you should start choosing your classes. That way you may have enough time to carefully select which classes you need to take to continue onward with your studies.

Also once you are enrolled in your classes most professor have their syllabus available online. It is suggested that students take a moment to read through the syllabus before the semester begins so they know what is expected of them for that course. You may even evaluate ahead of time if you happen to need a change of class or to even drop a class.

It is never too early to start looking at colleges you would like to attend. Start by creating a list of your hobbies, interests, and majors you would be interested in studying. From the list find 12 schools whether it be international, national, or local whichever is best fit for you. Once you found the 12 narrow it down to 6 colleges. This will be a reference to look back on during junior year to know where you are interested in going and make picking the right college an easier process. A website we found helpful with chosing college is Floridashines.org.

Once you complete the list, take your hobbies and interests and look for school clubs to get involved in. College admissions look for well-rounded students who succeed in academics and extracurricular courses. Plus if you start a sport or club freshman year there is room for growth within the organization and you can learn life-time skills.

A tip for always putting your mental health first.

Mental health typically affects the persons' behaviour, thinking, feeling and mood. Most college students tend to forget about their mental health because they're too focus about schooling and other things. "1 in 5 U.S. adults experience mental health illness each year."
