Guidance to College Readines

Are you a freshman in highschool who is starting to think about college? A student who feels scared to dive into the "adult" world. A student who needs a bit of guidance well this is checklist for you! You will be provided with tips/tricks to ease the college experience. At the end you will be to download a PNG version of the checklist to keep with you through your college prep journey.

Start by speaking with your advisor about the possibility of taking accelerated math courses. Studies have shown students who partake in higher level, rigorous courses are more likely to further their academic succes in college. Consider taking Alegbra 1 to then move onto to a calculus track to prepare.

Not only is it a good idea for you to discuss math courses but talk about Advance Placement classes too. These are college level courses applicable for college credit if you pass with a "3" or higher. Many college and universities recognize the credits which go towards earning your associates degree (AA) in a shorter time-frame.

Advisor can collaborate make sure that all students have equitable access to a college counseling program that encourages the culture of going to college. Speaking with your advisor is highly recommanded because theyre the ones who knows all the information about your major, like what kind of classes youll need to take. Every semester you should schedule a meeting with your advisor, so you can stay on track with your classes. You dont want to spend extra money on classes.

After speaking with your advisor set up a meeting with a tutor. This will be benefical if you find the coursework to be a bit of a challenge. A tutor will assit you not only with assignments but provide you with additional tools for overall structure. This is a great resource to prepare for college!

It is important for students to choose their classes before their enrollment date. Atleast a week or two prior to your enrollment date you should start choosing your classes. That way you may have enough time to carefully select which classes you need to take to continue onward with your studies.

Also once you are enrolled in your classes most professor have their syllabus available online. It is suggested that students take a moment to read through the syllabus before the semester begins so they know what is expected of them for that course. You may even evaluate ahead of time if you happen to need a change of class or to even drop a class.

It is never too early to start looking at colleges you would like to attend. Start by creating a list of your hobbies, interests, and majors you would be interested in studying. From the list find 12 schools whether it be international, national, or local whichever is best fit for you. Once you found the 12 narrow it down to 6 colleges. This will be a reference to look back on during junior year to know where you are interested in going and make picking the right college for you an easier process.

After the list take your hobbies and interests and look for school clubs to get involved in. College admissions look for well-rounded students who succeed in academics and extracurricular courses. Plus if you start a sport or club freshman year there is room for growth within the organization and you can learn life-time skills.
